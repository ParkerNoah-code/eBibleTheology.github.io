Greek Vocabulary


Feminine Nouns (ἡ)

ἀγάπη, ἡ — love
γῆ, ἡ — earth, land, ground (geology)
ζωή, ἡ — life (zoo, zoology)
φωνή, ἡ — voice, sound (phonetics, phonograph, phone)
ἀλήθεια, ἡ — truth
ἁμαρτία, ἡ — sin (hamartiology—the theological study of sin)
βασιλεία, ἡ — kingdom, reign (basilica)
δόξα, ἡ — glory, majesty (doxology)
ἐκκλησία, ἡ — congregation, assembly, church (ecclesiastical)
ἡμέρα, ἡ — day (ephemeral, "for a day")
καρδία, ἡ — heart (cardiologist)

Particles and Articles

δέ — and, but, now
καί — and, even, also
μέν — on the one hand, indeed
ὁ, ἡ, τό — the

Masculine Nouns (ὁ)

ἀδελφός, ὁ — brother (and sister) (Philadelphia, the city of brotherly love)
ἄνθρωπος, ὁ — man, human being, husband (anthropology, anthropomorphic)
θεός, ὁ — God (theology)
κόσμος, ὁ — world, universe; adornment (cosmos, cosmology, cosmetics)
κύριος, ὁ — Lord, master, sir
λόγος, ὁ — word, message, account (logic, theology, psychology)
νόμος, ὁ — law, principle (theonomists)
οὐρανός, ὁ — heaven, sky (Uranus)
υἱός, ὁ — son, descendant
Χριστός, ὁ — Christ, Messiah, Anointed One (Christ)

Neuter Nouns (τό)

ἔργον, τό — work, deed (ergonomic)
εὐαγγέλιον, τό — good news, gospel (evangelistic, evangelical)
ἱερόν, τό — temple, sanctuary
σημεῖον, τό — sign (semiotics)
τέκνον, τό — child, son, descendant

Case Functions

Nominative = Subject
Genitive = Possession or relationship
Dative = Indirect object
Accusative = Direct object
