Romans 1:18-3:20

Outline

Romans 1:18-32

Paul begins his argument by exposing the sinfulness of the Gentile world:

Romans 1:18 – "For the wrath of God is revealed from heaven against all ungodliness and unrighteousness of men, who by their unrighteousness suppress the truth."

Romans 1:19-20 – They have knowledge of God through general revelation but reject Him.

Romans 1:21-23 – Their rejection leads to idolatry.

Romans 1:24-32 – God gives them over to deeper sin, including sexual immorality, dishonorable passions, and a debased mind.

Romans 2:1-29

He then turns to the Jews, showing that they, too, are guilty of sin despite having the Law:

Romans 2:1-5 – Paul rebukes those who judge others while committing the same sins. This hypocrisy reveals that their religious identity has not led to true transformation. Instead of being exempt from judgment, they are storing up God’s wrath against themselves.

Romans 2:6-11 – Paul introduces God’s standard of impartial judgment. Both Jews and Gentiles are held to the same standard, emphasizing that outward privilege does not guarantee righteousness. This sets the stage for his later conclusion that all are guilty before God (Romans 3:9-20).

Romans 2:12-16 – Gentiles are accountable to the law “written on their hearts” while Jews are accountable to the written Law.

Romans 2:17-24 – Paul addresses Jews who rely on the Law and boast in their special relationship with God. They take pride in their knowledge of the Law and their role as guides for the blind, yet they fail to practice what they teach. Their hypocrisy dishonors God, leading Gentiles to blaspheme His name.

Romans 2:25-29 – Paul challenges the Jewish belief that physical circumcision guarantees righteousness. True circumcision is not outward but inward—of the heart, by the Spirit, not merely by the letter of the Law. A true Jew is one whose heart is transformed, not just someone with an external religious identity. Ultimately, God's praise is for those who are inwardly renewed, not merely outwardly observant.
