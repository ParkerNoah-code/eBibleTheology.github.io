Lesson 5: Present Indicative Verbs

1. Present Tense Uses

  • Progressive Present: Ongoing action. Example: πάντες ζητοῦσίν σε ("all are looking for you").
  • Iterative Present: Repeated or continuous action. Example: πᾶς ὁ ἐν αὐτῷ μένων οὐχ ἁμαρτάνει ("everyone who abides in him does not continue in sin").
  • Historical Present: Past event described as present. Example: καὶ ἔρχονται φέροντες πρὸς αὐτὸν παραλυτικὸν ("and they came to him bringing a paralytic").

2. Verb Morphology

Structure: Stem + Connecting Vowel + Personal Ending

Example: λυ + ο + μεν → λύομεν ("we are loosing").

3. Present Active Indicative Endings

Singular Plural
-ω (I) -ομεν (we)
-εις (you sg.) -ετε (you pl.)
-ει (he/she/it) -ουσι(ν) (they)

4. Present Middle/Passive Indicative Endings

Singular Plural
-ομαι (I) -όμεθα (we)
-ῃ (you sg.) -εσθε (you pl.)
-εται (he/she/it) -ονται (they)

5. Connecting Vowels

  • ο (omicron) before μ, ν.
  • ε (epsilon) before other letters.
  • Examples: λύομεν, λύετε.

6. Mood

The indicative mood asserts a fact or asks a definite question.

  • ἔχομεν = "we have" (indicative)
  • ἔχωμεν = "let us have" (subjunctive)

7. Parsing Formula

Parsing provides full information about a verb:

  1. Lexical form
  2. Tense (Pres)
  3. Voice (Active, Middle, Passive)
  4. Mood (Indicative)
  5. Person (1st, 2nd, 3rd)
  6. Number (Singular, Plural)

Examples:

  • πορεύῃ → πορεύομαι, Pres, Middle, Indicative, 2nd person, Singular = "you are going."
  • λύουσιν → λύω, Pres, Active, Indicative, 3rd person, Plural = "they are loosing."

8. Crasis (Word Merger)

Example: καί + ἐγώ → κἀγώ ("and I").

9. Vocabulary

  • βαπτίζω – I baptize, immerse, dip (baptize)
  • θεραπεύω – I heal (therapeutic)
  • κράζω – I cry out
  • ἄγγελος, ὁ – angel, messenger (angel)
  • μαθητής, ὁ – disciple, follower (mathematics)
  • ὄχλος, ὁ – crowd
  • προφήτης, ὁ – prophet (prophet)
  • γάρ – for, because
  • ἐκεῖ – there, in that place
  • κἀγώ – and I (crasis)
  • οὖν – then, so, therefore
  • οὕτως – thus, so
  • τέ – and, but
  • οὐ, οὐκ, οὐχ – no, not
  • οὐχί – emphatic no!
