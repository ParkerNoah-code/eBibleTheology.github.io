Lesson 4: Overview of Verbs

I. Basics of Greek Verbs

  • Person
  • Number
  • Voice
  • Mood
  • Tense & Aspect

II. Person

  • 1st person: subject speaking ("I", "we")
  • 2nd person: subject spoken to ("you")
  • 3rd person: subject spoken about ("he/she/it", "they")

III. Number

  • Singular
  • Plural

IV. Voice

  • Active: subject performs action
  • Middle: subject performs and is affected by action
  • Passive: subject receives action

Middle Voice Categories

  • Reflexive
  • Special Interest
  • Reciprocity
  • Movement
  • Self-Involvement
  • Passivity

V. Mood

  • Indicative: certain/asserted
  • Subjunctive: probable/contingent
  • Optative: possible/hoped for
  • Imperative: command/request

VI. Tense & Aspect

Tenses

Tense Form Translation
Present λύω I am loosing / I loose
Imperfect ἔλυον I was loosing
Future λύσω I will loose
Aorist ἔλυσα I loosed
Perfect λέλυκα I have loosed
Pluperfect ἐλελύκειν I had loosed

Aspects

  • Imperfective Aspect: present and imperfect; ongoing action
  • Perfective Aspect: aorist; complete action
  • Stative Aspect: perfect and pluperfect; resulting state

VII. Present Indicative of εἰμί

Person Singular Plural
1st εἰμί ἐσμέν
2nd εἶ ἐστέ
3rd ἐστίν εἰσίν

VIII. Vocabulary

Verbs

  • ἄγω - I lead, bring
  • βλέπω - I see, look at
  • διδάσκω - I teach
  • ἐγείρω - I raise up
  • κρίνω - I judge, condemn
  • μένω - I remain, abide, dwell
  • ὑπάγω - I go away, depart

Nouns

  • δοῦλος, ὁ - slave
  • θάνατος, ὁ - death
  • ψυχή, ἡ - soul, life, living being
  • ὥρα, ἡ - hour

Particles / Conjunctions

  • εἰ - if, whether
  • εἴτε - if, whether
  • καθώς - as, just as, even as
  • ὡς - as, like
