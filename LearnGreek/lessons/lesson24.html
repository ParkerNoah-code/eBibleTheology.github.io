Lesson 24: MI Verbs

Key Characteristics

  • MI verbs are an older Greek conjugation; present active indicative ends in -μι.
  • No connecting vowel; stems end in vowel:
    • δίδωμι → δο-
    • τίθημι → θε-
    • ἵστημι → στα-
  • Old reduplication uses ι instead of ε; always present in the present tense.

δίδωμι Present Active Indicative

Singular Plural
δίδωμι (1st) δίδομεν (1st)
δίδως (2nd) δίδοτε (2nd)
δίδωσι(ν) (3rd) διδόασι(ν) (3rd)

Note: Singular forms have long vowels (ω); plural forms have short vowels (ο).

Aorist Forms

  • Present reduplication disappears.
  • δίδωμι and τίθημι use kappa aorist; ἵστημι uses both 1st and 2nd aorist.
Verb Aorist
δίδωμι ἔδωκα
τίθημι ἔθηκα
ἵστημι ἔστησα (1st aor), ἔστην (2nd aor)

Present and Aorist Forms Comparison

Present MI Forms

  • διδόμενος – Pres, Act, Ptc, m/n sg
  • διδούς – Pres, Act, Ptc, m sg
  • διδῷ – Pres, Act, Subj, 3 sg
  • διδότω – Pres, Act, Impv, 3 sg
  • διδόναι – Pres, Act, Inf

Aorist MI Forms

  • δοθείς – Aor, Pass, Ptc, m/n sg
  • δούς – Aor, Act, Ptc, m sg
  • δῷ – Aor, Act, Subj, 3 sg
  • δότω – Aor, Act, Impv, 3 sg
  • δοῦναι – Aor, Act, Inf

ἵστημι Aorist Note

  • ἔστησα = transitive, 1st aor (“I stood [something] up”)
  • ἔστην = intransitive, 2nd aor (“I stood up”)

ἀφίημι Present Active Indicative

Singular Plural
ἀφίημι (1st) ἀφίομεν (1st)
ἀφίης (2nd) ἀφίετε (2nd)
ἀφίησιν (3rd) ἀφίασιν (3rd)

Note: Singular forms follow MI verbs; plural forms follow ω-verbs.

Identification Tip

  • δοθείσης = Aor, Pass, Ptc, f, g, sg.
  • From δίδωμι (δο- stem → aorist, passive, no augment = non-indicative).
  • Attributive participle: “which was given.”
