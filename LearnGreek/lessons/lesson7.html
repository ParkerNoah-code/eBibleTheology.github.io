Lesson 7: Contract Verbs

Contract Verbs

Contract verbs are verbs whose stem ends in α, ε, ο. Contraction occurs when the stem vowel meets the connecting vowel from the ending. The vowels ι (iota) and υ (upsilon) remain unaffected.

Contraction Rules

Alpha Contract Verbs (α)

  • α + ε, η → α
  • α + ει, ῃ → ᾳ
  • α + ο, ου, ω → ω

Epsilon Contract Verbs (ε)

  • ε + ε, ει → ει
  • ε + ῃ → ῃ
  • ε + ο, ου → ου
  • ε + ω → ω
  • ε + η → η

Omicron Contract Verbs (ο)

  • ο + ε, ο, ου → ου
  • ο + ει, ῃ → οι
  • ο + η, ω → ω

Contraction occurs only in the present and imperfect tenses. Other tenses (future, aorist, perfect) add a consonant (σ or κ) to prevent contraction.

Present tense contract verbs are accented with a circumflex over the contracted syllable.

Epsilon contract verbs are the most common in the New Testament (700+ occurrences).

Paradigm of ποιέω (to do, to make)

Person Singular Plural
1st ποιῶ ποιοῦμεν
2nd ποιεῖς ποιεῖτε
3rd ποιεῖ ποιοῦσιν

Note: The lexical form ποιέω is uncontracted and is only used to show the paradigm.

Imperfect Indicative of εἰμί (to be)

Person Singular Plural
1st ἤμην ἦμεν
2nd ἦς ἦτε
3rd ἦν ἦσαν

Alternate Direct Object Cases

Some verbs take direct objects in the genitive or dative instead of the accusative. The genitive is not translated as "of", nor the dative as "to/for".

Genitive Direct Object

  • ἀκούσουσιν τῆς φωνῆς – "They hear the voice"
  • ἐκράτησεν τῆς χειρὸς αὐτῆς – "He grasped her hand"
  • ἐπληροῦντο χαρᾶς – "They were filled with joy"

Verbs: ἀκούω, ἄρχω, κρατέω, πληρόω

Dative Direct Object

  • ἀκολουθοῦσιν αὐτῷ – "They are following him"
  • ἀποκρίνεται αὐτοῖς – "He answers them"
  • οὐ πιστεύετέ μοι – "You do not believe me"
  • προσεκύνη αὐτῷ – "He was worshipping him"

Verbs: ἀκολουθέω, ἀποκρίνομαι, πιστεύω, προσκυνέω

  • εἰς (acc) – into, among, for

  • ἐν (dat) – in, on, at, by, with

  • κατά (gen) – down, against; (acc) – according to
  • μετά (gen) – with, among; (acc) – after
  • παρά (gen) – from; (dat) – with; (acc) – beside, at
  • περί (gen) – about, concerning; (acc) – around
  • πρός (acc) – to, toward
  • σύν (dat) – with
