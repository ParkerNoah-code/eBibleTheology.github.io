Lesson 23: Imperative Mood

1. Imperative Mood Basics

  • Functions: command, prohibition, request.
  • Occurs only in present and aorist tense forms (rarely perfect; no imperfect, future, or pluperfect).
  • No 1st person imperative; 1st person plural subjunctive used instead.

3. Uses of Imperatives

  • Command: διώκετε (pursue!), ἀκούέτω (let him hear!)
  • Prohibition: μή + present imperative → μὴ ἀγαπᾶτε (do not love)
  • Request: ἐλθέτω (let ... come), βοήθησον (help us)

4. Subjunctives as Imperatives

  • 1st person plural exhortation: ἀγαπῶμεν (let us love one another)
  • Prohibitory:
    • Present imperative + μή: μὴ λυέ (don’t loose)
    • 2nd person aorist subjunctive: μὴ λύσῃς (don’t loose)

5. Verbal Aspect in Imperatives

  • Aorist (perfective): κρίνετε (judge him once, specific occasion)
  • Present (imperfective): μὴ κρίνετε (do not keep judging, general practice)

6. Optative Mood

  • Function: wish (volitive mood), rare in NT (68 occurrences)
  • Common forms:
    • μὴ γένοιτο = may it never be! (17x)
    • γένοιτο = 3 sg optative of γίνομαι
    • εἴη = 3 sg optative of εἰμί

7. Vocabulary

Verbs

  • ἀνίστημι – I stand up, arise
  • ἀποδίδωμι – I give back, pay
  • ἀπόλλυμι – I destroy, am lost
  • ἀφίημι – I forgive, let go, divorce
  • δίδωμι – I give
  • ἵστημι – I stand, set
  • παραδίδωμι – I hand over, betray, entrust
  • τίθημι – I put, place, appoint
  • φημί – I say, affirm

Nouns/Adjectives

  • ἁμαρτωλός – sinful, sinner
  • γενεά – generation, family
  • μικρός – small
  • ὅμοιος – similar, same nature

Adverbs/Particles

  • ἄχρι – until
  • οὐαί – woe
