Lesson 3: Second Declension Nouns

Second Declension Essentials

Number

  • English: -s, -es (boys, boxes), vowel changes (man → men).
  • Greek: Applies across masculine and neuter forms.

Gender

  • First Declension (Feminine): φωνή, καρδία
  • Second Declension (Masculine): λόγος
  • Second Declension (Neuter): τέκνον

Case Functions

Case Function
Nominative Subject
Genitive Possession
Dative Indirect Object
Accusative Direct Object
Vocative Direct Address

Second Declension Masculine Endings

Case Singular Plural
Nominative -ος -οι
Genitive -ου -ων
Dative -ῳ -οις
Accusative -ον -ους
Vocative -ε —

The masculine and neuter genitive plural share the ending -ων (same as 1st declension feminine).

Second Declension Neuter Endings

Case Singular Plural
Nominative -ον -α
Genitive -ου -ων
Dative -ῳ -οις
Accusative -ον -α
Vocative -ον -α

Neuter nominative and accusative forms have identical endings.

Vocative Case

Used for direct address. Notable masculine singular forms:

  • κύριε – Lord
  • υἱέ – son
  • ὦ ἄνθρωπε – O man!

Proper Nouns

  • Capitalized in Greek: e.g., τῷ Ἰσραήλ (“to Israel”).
  • Some decline; foreign names often do not.
  • The article communicates case when the noun does not decline.
  • Article with proper names is not translated: ὁ Ἰωσὴφ = Joseph.
