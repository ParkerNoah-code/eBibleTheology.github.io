Lesson 11: 1st Aorist Verbs

Meaning

  • The 1st aorist expresses a simple past event (“I loosed”).
  • Greek: ἀόριστος (“without boundaries”, “not defined”).
  • Presents the action as a whole without focus on beginning, end, or duration.

Uses of the Aorist

  1. Constative Aorist: Summarizes action as a whole (e.g., ἐβασίλευσεν, Romans 5:14).
  2. Inceptive Aorist: Emphasizes beginning of a state or action (e.g., ἐδάκρυσεν, John 11:35).
  3. Epistolary Aorist: Used for author’s past action, reader’s present (e.g., ἔπεμψα, Ephesians 6:22).

1st Aorist Morphology

Formation (example: ἐλύσαμεν)

  1. Augment (ε + stem)
  2. Stem (λύ–)
  3. Connecting vowel (σ)
  4. Personal endings

Personal Endings

Active

  • σα, σας, σε(ν), σαμεν, σατε, σαν

Middle

  • σάμην, σω, σατο, σάμεθα, σασθε, σαντο

Passive

  • θην, θης, θη, θημεν, θητε, θησαν

Augment Rules

  • Consonant stem: add ε–
  • Vowel stem: lengthen to η or ω (e.g., ἀκούω → ἤκουσα)
  • Diphthong αι–: αἰτεῖτε → ᾐτήσατε

Stem Variations

Stem Type Consonants + σ → Result Example
Dentals δ, ζ, θ, τ σ ἐδόξασα
Palatals γ, κ, σκ, σσ, χ ξ ἐδίωξα
Labials π, β, πτ, φ ψ ἔβλεψα

Note: Contract verbs usually lengthen the contract vowel (e.g., ἠγαπήθην).

Vocabulary

  • ἀναβαίνω – I go up, ascend
  • ἀπέρχομαι – I go away, depart
  • ἀποθνῄσκω – I die
  • βάλλω – I throw, cast out
  • εἰσέρχομαι – I go in, enter
  • ἐκβάλλω – I drive/send out
  • ἐσθίω – I eat
  • ἐξέρχομαι – I go out, depart, leave
  • εὑρίσκω – I find, discover
  • καταβαίνω – I go down, descend
  • πίνω – I drink
  • πίπτω – I fall
  • προσέρχομαι – I go to, approach
  • φέρω – I bear, carry
  • ἐπαγγελία, ἡ – promise
