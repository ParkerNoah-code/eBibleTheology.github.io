Lesson 1: The Alphabet

Greek Alphabet

Lower Upper Name Sound
α Α Alpha ah (father)
β Β Beta b (ball)
γ Γ Gamma g (gift)
δ Δ Delta d (dog)
ε Ε Epsilon eh (echo)
ζ Ζ Zeta dz (kudzu)
η Η Eta ay (ate)
θ Θ Theta th (thin)
ι Ι Iota ee (ski)
κ Κ Kappa k (key)
λ Λ Lambda l (lock)
μ Μ Mu m (mom)
ν Ν Nu n (nail)
ξ Ξ Xi ks (fox)
ο Ο Omicron aw (often)
π Π Pi p (pond)
ρ Ρ Rho r (rhyme)
σ / ς Σ Sigma s (sand)
τ Τ Tau t (tap)
υ Υ Upsilon oo (boot)
φ Φ Phi ph (phone)
χ Χ Chi ch (ache)
ψ Ψ Psi ps (oops)
ω Ω Omega ōh (obey)

Special Sigma Forms

  • σ: used at beginning/middle of words
  • ς: used at end of words

Pronunciation Clusters

  • γγ = ng (ring)
  • γκ = ngk (drink)
  • γχ = ngch (anchor)
  • γ → ν, ζ → ξ, θ → φ

Common Letter Confusions

  • ν → υ
  • ο → σ
  • π → τ
  • φ → ψ
  • η → n
  • ρ → p
  • υ → u
  • χ → x
  • ω → w

Vowels

  • Short: ε, ο
  • Long: η, ω
  • Short/Long: α, ι, υ

Iota Subscript (Not Pronounced)

  • ᾳ = ah (καρδίᾳ)
  • ῃ = ay (ἀγάπῃ)
  • ῳ = oh (λόγῳ)

Diphthongs

Pair Sound
αι eye (aisle)
αυ ow (kraut)
ει ay (freight)
ευ yew (feud)
οι oi (oil)
ου oo (soup)
υι we (suite)

Diaeresis (Separate Vowel Sounds)

  • αϊ = ah-ee (Κάϊν)
  • ωϋ = ō-oo (Μωϋσῆς)
  • ιη = ee-ay → yea (Ἰησοῦς)

Breathing Marks

  • Smooth: ἀ = vowel only
  • Rough: ἁ = h-sound added
  • Rho (ῥ) always takes rough breathing
  • Diphthongs: mark over second vowel

Accent Marks

  • Acute (ά): last 3 syllables
  • Circumflex (ᾶ): last 2 syllables (long)
  • Grave (ὰ): last syllable only

Accent Position Rules

  • Syllables: antepenult → penult → ultima
  • If ultima is long, accent shifts forward

Punctuation

Greek Meaning
. period
, comma
· semicolon
; question mark

Vocabulary

  • ἀγάπη, ἡ – love
  • γῆ, ἡ – earth, land (geo-)
  • ζωή, ἡ – life (zoo-)
  • φωνή, ἡ – voice, sound (phono-)
  • ἀλήθεια, ἡ – truth
  • ἁμαρτία, ἡ – sin (hamartiology)
  • βασιλεία, ἡ – kingdom (basilica)
  • δόξα, ἡ – glory (doxology)
  • ἐκκλησία, ἡ – church, assembly (ecclesiastical)
  • ἡμέρα, ἡ – day (ephemeral)
  • καρδία, ἡ – heart (cardiology)
  • δέ – and, but, now
  • καί – and, even, also
  • μέν – on the one hand, indeed
  • ὁ, ἡ, τό – the (articles)
