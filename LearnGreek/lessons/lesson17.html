Lesson 17: Present Participles

1. Definition

A participle is a verbal adjective. Greek participles have:

  • Tense (present, aorist, perfect)
  • Voice (active, middle, passive)
  • Mood (participle)
  • Gender, case, number

2. General Facts

  • Masculine forms occur 7× more than feminine and neuter combined.
  • 1 in 20 words in the Greek NT is a participle.
  • No imperfect participles; only 13 future participles in the NT.

3. Present Active Participles

Form = Stem + ο (vowel) + ντ (infix) + adjectival ending.

The infix -ντ- signals active voice. Masculine and neuter follow third declension endings.

Masculine Example: λύω

Case Singular Plural
Nominative λύων λύοντες
Genitive λύοντος λυόντων
Dative λύοντι λύουσι(ν)
Accusative λύοντα λύοντας

4. Present Middle/Passive Participles

Form = Stem + ο (vowel) + μεν (infix) + adjectival ending.

The infix -μεν- indicates middle or passive voice. Forms follow first and second declension endings.

Masculine Example: λύω

Case Singular Plural
Nominative λυόμενος λυόμενοι
Genitive λυομένου λυομένων
Dative λυομένῳ λυομένοις
Accusative λυόμενον λυομένους

5. Contract Verbs

  • καλέω → καλοῦντος
  • πληρόω → πληρούμενον
  • ἀγαπάω → ἀγαπῶντι

6. Parsing Order

Participle → Lexical Form → Tense → Voice → Mood → Gender → Case → Number

Example: λυομέναις = Present, Middle/Passive, Participle, Feminine, Dative, Plural.

7. Uses of Participles

Adjectival Positions

  1. Attributive: modifies expressed noun.
    Example: ὁ πατήρ ὁ βλέπων = "the father who sees"
  2. Substantive: modifies unexpressed noun.
    Example: ὁ βλέπων = "the one who sees"
  3. Adverbial: modifies main verb, often contemporaneous action.
    Example: εἶπεν διδάσκων = "he said while teaching"

8. Voice Markers

Active Participles

  • Masculine/Neuter: -οντ- (e.g., λύοντος)
  • Feminine: -ουσ- (e.g., λυούσης)

Middle/Passive Participles

  • Masculine: λυόμενος
  • Feminine: λυομένη
  • Neuter: λυόμενον

9. Aspect

Participles communicate kind of action, not time.

  • Imperfective: Present (action in progress)
  • Perfective: Aorist (action viewed as a whole)
  • Stative: Perfect (completed action with continuing result)

10. Vocabulary

  • κρατέω – I grasp, seize, arrest
