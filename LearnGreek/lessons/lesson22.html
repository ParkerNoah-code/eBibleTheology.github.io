Lesson 22: The Subjunctive Mood

Meaning

  • Subjunctive mood expresses potential or indefinite action (not actual).
  • Present and aorist subjunctives do not indicate time; tense indicates aspect:
    • Present: progressive or repeated action.
    • Aorist: simple or undefined action.
  • Subjunctive actions are usually future-oriented.

Morphology & Paradigms

Subjunctive Tenses

  • Present and Aorist only.
  • No Perfect Subjunctive in New Testament Greek.

Present Active Subjunctive Endings (λύω paradigm)

Person Ending
1st singular ω
2nd singular ῃς
3rd singular ῃ
1st plural ωμεν
2nd plural ητε
3rd plural ωσι(ν)

Present Middle/Passive Subjunctive Endings

Person Ending
1st singular ωμαι
2nd singular ῃ
3rd singular ηται
1st plural ώμεθα
2nd plural ησθε
3rd plural ωνται

Special Forms

  • Aorist Active: same as present active + σ to stem.
  • Aorist Middle: present M/P endings + σ.
  • Aorist Passive: θ replaces σ (with circumflex accent).
  • Present Active εἰμί: ὦ, ᾖς, ᾖ, ὦμεν, ἦτε, ὦσιν.

Irregular Forms

  • 2nd Aorist: λαβῇ, γένηται
  • Contract Verbs:
    • ποιῇ → ποιήσῃ
    • ἀγαπᾷ → ἀγαπήσῃ
  • Liquid Verbs:
    • μένῃ → μείνῃ
    • ἀποστέλλῃ → ἀποστείλῃ

Uses

  1. Purpose or Result Clauses
    • ἵνα (purpose)
    • ὅπως (result)
  2. Indefinite Clauses
    • ὅς ἄν (whoever)
    • ὅταν, ἕως ἄν (whenever, until)
  3. Emphatic Negation: οὐ μή = absolutely not
  4. Deliberative: real or rhetorical questions
  5. Conditional Clauses
    • ἐάν (if)
    • ἐὰν μή (unless)

Vocabulary

  • ἀσπάζομαι – I greet
  • ἐπιγινώσκω – I know, understand
  • ἐργάζομαι – I work, perform
  • καθίζω – I sit
  • κατοικέω – I dwell
  • χαίρω – I rejoice; greetings
  • ἔτος, -ους, τό – year
  • θηρίον, τό – animal, beast
  • θλῖψις, -εως, ἡ – tribulation
  • χρεία, ἡ – need
  • κρίσις, -εως, ἡ – judgment
  • φόβος, ὁ – fear, reverence
  • φυλακή, ἡ – watch, guard, prison
  • ἔμπροσθεν – before
  • ἰδού – behold, see
