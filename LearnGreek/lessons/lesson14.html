Lesson 14: Third Declension Nouns

Third Declension Basics

  • Stem ends in a consonant.
  • Gender can be masculine, feminine, or neuter (determined by the article).

Articles

  • Masculine: ὁ
  • Feminine: ἡ
  • Neuter: τὸ

Case Endings

Masculine and Feminine

Case Singular Plural
Nominative –ς / – –ες
Genitive –ος –ων
Dative –ι –σι(ν)
Accusative –α –ας

Neuter

Case Singular Plural
Nominative – –α
Genitive –ος –ων
Dative –ι –σι(ν)
Accusative – –α
  • Neuter nominative and accusative forms are identical.
  • Nouns ending in –μα are always neuter (largest group).
  • Dative plural may have moveable nu (ν).

Stem Recognition

  • Nominative singular forms often undergo spelling changes.
  • Genitive singular forms show the unaltered stem plus ending.
  • Always memorize both the nominative and genitive singular forms.

Examples

  • σαρκ + ς → σάρξ
  • σαρκ + ος → σαρκός

Vocabulary

Verbs

  • ἐγγίζω – I approach, draw near
  • ζάω – I live
  • οἶδα – I know, understand
  • ὁράω – I see, perceive
  • πείθω – I persuade, convince
