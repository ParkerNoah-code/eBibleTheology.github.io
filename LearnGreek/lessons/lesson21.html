Lesson 21: Infinitives

Definition

  • Infinitive = verbal noun.
  • Has aspect (perfective, imperfective, stative) and voice (active, middle, passive).
  • Can take a direct object and be modified by an adverb.
  • Negated by μή.
  • Subject of infinitive is in the accusative case.
  • Takes neuter singular article: τό, τοῦ, τῷ.
  • Only one form (not limited by person or number).

Forms and Paradigms

Present Infinitives

Form Translation
λύειν to be loosing
λύεσθαι to be loosed

Aorist Infinitives

Form Translation
λῦσαι to loose
λύσασθαι to loose oneself
λυθῆναι to be loosed

2nd Aorist Infinitives

Verb Infinitive
ἄγω ἀγαγεῖν
λέγω εἰπεῖν
γίνομαι γενέσθαι
ἔρχομαι ἐλθεῖν
λαμβάνω λαβεῖν
ὁράω ἰδεῖν

Perfect Infinitives

Form Translation
λελυκέναι to have loosed
λελύσθαι to have been loosed

Uses of the Infinitive

  1. Complementary: completes the idea of another verb (e.g., θέλω, δύναμαι, μέλλω).
  2. Purpose: infinitive alone, or with τοῦ, εἰς τὸ.
  3. Result: typically after ὥστε.
  4. Previous Time: μετά τὸ + infinitive (action before main verb).
  5. Contemporaneous Time: ἐν τῷ + infinitive (action at same time).
  6. Subsequent Time: πρό τοῦ or πρίν(ἤ) + infinitive (action after main verb).
  7. Causal: διὰ τὸ + infinitive ("because").
  8. Substantival: functions as subject or object (e.g., τὸ ζῆν, τὸ ἀποθανεῖν).

Verbal Aspect

  • Infinitives convey aspect, not tense.
  • Present: ongoing process (e.g., διδάσκειν = to be teaching).
  • Aorist: simple action (e.g., διδάξαι = to teach).

Irregular Verbs: δύναμαι

Person Form
1st sg. δύναμαι
2nd sg. δύνᾳ
3rd sg. δύναται
1st pl. δυνάμεθα
2nd pl. δύνασθε
3rd pl. δύνανται

Imperfect: ἐδύνατο or ἠδύνατο.

Vocabulary

  • δέχομαι – I take, receive
  • δικαιόω – I justify
  • λογίζομαι – I reckon
  • σταυρόω – I crucify
  • ἄν – particle of indefiniteness
  • ἐάν – if, when
  • ἕως – until, while
  • ἵνα – in order that
  • ὅπως, ὅταν, ὅτε – when, that
  • ποῦ – where?
