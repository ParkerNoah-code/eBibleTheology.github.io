Lesson 18: Aorist Participles

1. General Features

  • Participle = verbal adjective (tense + voice + adjective form).
  • Aorist participles use σα (active/middle) or θε (passive) plus endings.

2. Aorist Active Participles

Example: λύω → λύσας (“having loosed”)

Masculine Singular

  • λύσας
  • λύσαντος
  • λύσαντι
  • λύσαντα

Feminine Singular

  • λύσασα
  • λυσάσης
  • λυσάσῃ
  • λύσασαν

Neuter Singular

  • λύσαν
  • λύσαντος
  • λύσαντι
  • λύσαν

3. Aorist Middle Participles

Form: stem + σα + μεν + endings.

Example: λυσάμενος (“having loosed for oneself”)

  • λυσάμενος
  • λυσαμένου
  • λυσαμένῳ
  • λυσάμενον

4. Aorist Passive Participles

Form: stem + θε + endings.

Example: λυθείς (“having been loosed”)

  • λυθείς
  • λυθέντος
  • λυθέντι
  • λυθέντα

5. 2nd Aorist Participles

  • Same endings as present participles.
  • No augment; only stem changes.

Examples

  • γίνομαι → γενόμενος (“having become”)
  • λαμβάνω → λαβών (“having received”)

6. Uses of Aorist Participles

Attributive

Article + participle; example:

  • ὁ ἐλθών = “the one who came”

Substantival

Participle with implied noun:

  • ὁ καλέσας = “the one who called”

Adverbial

No article; often temporal:

  • ἐλθών = “after he came”

7. Negation

  • Indicative verbs: οὐ (οὐκ, οὐχ)
  • Non-indicative forms (participles, etc.): μή

8. Numbers in Greek

1–3 Singular Forms

Case Masculine Feminine Neuter
Nominative εἷς μία ἕν
Genitive ἑνός μιᾶς ἑνός
Dative ἑνί μιᾷ ἑνί
Accusative ἕνα μίαν ἕν

3 Plural Forms

Case Masculine/Feminine Neuter
Nominative τρεῖς τρία
Genitive τριῶν τριῶν
Dative τρισί(ν) τρισί(ν)
Accusative τρεῖς τρία

Notes:

  • 1–4 decline; higher numbers do not.
  • εἷς / ἕν must be distinguished from εἰς (into) and ἐν (in).

9. Vocabulary

  • προσφέρω – I bring to, offer
  • ἑτοιμάζω – I prepare
  • δέω – I bind
  • παιδίον, τό – child
  • ὄρος, τό – mountain, hill
  • ἐλπίς, ἡ – hope
  • μόνος, -η, -ον – only, single, alone
  • ὅλος, -η, -ον – whole, entire, complete
  • ἀγαπητός, -ή, -όν – beloved
  • μέσος, -η, -ον – middle, midst
  • λοιπός, -ή, -όν – remaining, rest
  • δεξιός, -ά, -όν – right
  • ἄρα – so then
  • ἤδη – already, now
  • ὧδε – here
