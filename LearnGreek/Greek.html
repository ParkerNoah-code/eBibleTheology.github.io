Introduction to Greek

Purpose

This page outlines my approach to learning Biblical Greek. Below are the primary resources I am using, including textbooks, lecture series, and tools for practice and reading.

Primary Resources

I'm working towards reading 1 John initially. That requires beginning greek and then a little bit of vocabulary after that. The grammar I'm using is Beginning with New Testament Greek by Benjamin L Merkle and Robert L. Plummer. The website for the textbook Daily Dose of Greek has free lectures going over the material and other helpful resources.

For free materials, biblicalelearning.org is a great place to go. There is a Mastering New Testament Greek home page, Textbook, and Workbook. There is also a lecture series to go along with it:

The bundle of Danny Zacharias Apps (ParseGreek, FlashGreek, and IGreek) is great for parsing and vocab practice. The website mastergreek.com is a free alternative for parsing practice specifically.

I like Tyndale's physical Greek New Testament for reading. The Greek New Testament SBL Edition can be read online for free. Another option is reading the New Testament at gntreader.com which has Abbot-Smith's A Manual Greek Lexicon of the New Testament alongside of it.

Three video series to reference on 1 John: Greek Bible Speaker (Eras. Gk) by Ted Hildebrandt Biblicalelearning for listening to 1 John, 1 John 1 by Daily Dose of Greek for a quick translation guide, and 1 John in Greek by Ken Schenck for detailed translation explanation.

Overall Approach

The vocabulary in each section should be mastered. The paradigms should be drilled. Don't get stuck on a chapter if it is not quite mastered. Do the practice, work on memorizing, but keep moving through the material. Some of it will take time to sink in or will make more sense as you continue on.

Below is an outline of the material following the Beginning with New Testament Greek textbook and you can find topic by topic resources on the Notes Page:

Phase 1: Foundations

Master the Alphabet (Chapter 1): Acquire immediate familiarity with the Greek alphabet, including diacritical marks (breathing marks, accents). Practice writing, reading, and pronunciation.

First & Second Declension Nouns and Articles (Chapters 2–3): Grasp gender, number, and case paradigms for nouns and the definite article. Emphasis on nominative, genitive, dative, and accusative cases.

Make sure you start memorizing the vocabulary systematically.

Phase 2: Verbal System I – Present Tense Mastery

Basics of Greek Verbs (Chapter 4): Understand verb morphology, stems, personal endings, and the thematic vowel. Introduction to tense, voice, mood framework.

Master the Present Active Indicative (Chapter 5): Conjugate regular thematic verbs in the present active indicative.

Learn Imperfect Verbs (Chapter 6): Introduction to augment and secondary endings; formation and translation of the imperfect tense.

Tackle Contract Verbs (Chapter 7): Study verbs whose stems end in a vowel (α, ε, ο) and the phonological rules governing their conjugations.

Phase 3: Building Sentences

Prepositions and Cases (Chapter 8): Examine prepositional usage with various cases to modify nouns and verbs within sentence structures.

Personal and Relative Pronouns (Chapter 9): Learn paradigms and syntactical roles of personal pronouns (ἐγώ, σύ, αὐτός) and relative pronouns (ὅς, ἥ, ὅ).

Phase 4: Verbal System II – Expanding Tenses

Future Tense (Chapter 10): Introduce the temporal augment and sigma marker for regular future tense formation.

First and Second Aorist (Chapters 11–12): Differentiate between the first (sigmatic) and second (thematic) aorist; practice their indicative forms and principal parts.

Liquid Verbs (Chapter 13): Examine phonological changes in verbs with stems ending in λ, μ, ν, ρ when forming certain tenses.

Continue drilling vocabulary and parsing forms daily. Maintain consistent review and parsing practice to reinforce morphology and syntax recognition.

Phase 5: Nouns, Adjectives, Participles

Third Declension Nouns (Chapter 14): Study complex paradigms involving consonantal stem changes and irregular nominative forms.

Perfect and Pluperfect Verbs (Chapter 15): Explore reduplication, perfect active endings, and formation of the pluperfect indicative.

Adjectives and Adverbs (Chapter 16): Learn agreement rules for attributive and predicate adjectives; derivation of adverbs from adjective stems.

Begin Present Participles (Chapter 17): Analyze present participle morphology across all genders and voices.

Phase 6: Advanced Verbs and Syntax

Aorist and Perfect Participles (Chapters 18–19): Understand principal parts and function of participles in attributive and circumstantial clauses.

Other Pronouns (Chapter 20): Examine additional pronominal forms beyond personal and relative pronouns. Emphasis on declension patterns, syntactic function, and translation nuances.

Infinitives (Chapter 21), Subjunctives (Chapter 22), Imperatives & Optatives (Chapter 23): Master non-finite verbal forms and their syntactical uses in indirect discourse, purpose clauses, and commands.

μι-Verbs (Chapter 24): Study the unique paradigms of athematic verbs; focus on their complex conjugation patterns.

Plan to spend more time for this phase due to inherent irregularity and complexity.
