Introduction to Greek

Primary Resources

I'm working towards reading 1 John initially. That requires beginning greek and then a little bit of vocabulary after that. The grammar I'm using is Beginning with New Testament Greek by Benjamin L Merkle and Robert L. Plummer. The website for the textbook Daily Dose of Greek has free lectures going over the material and other helpful resources.

This is an additional place to go over the materials from the book besides the author's website:

For free materials, biblicalelearning.org has a lot of good resources. Check out the Mastering New Testament Greek home page, Textbook, and Workbook. This author also has a lecture series going over the material:

Check out mastergreek.com for free parsing practice.

Additionally, I have Tydale's physical Greek New Testament for reading. The Greek New Testament SBL Edition can be read online. Another option is reading the New Testament at gntreader.com alongside Abbot-Smith's A Manual Greek Lexicon of the New Testament.

Three video series to reference on 1 John: Greek Bible Speaker (Eras. Gk) by Ted Hildebrandt Biblicalelearning reading 1 John, 1 John 1 by Daily Dose of Greek for a quick translation guide, and 1 John in Greek by Ken Schenck for detailed explanation.

Secondary Resources

The bundle of Danny Zacharias Apps (ParseGreek, FlashGreek, and IGreek) is helpful for practice.

Bill Mounce has free lectures on biblicaltraining.org called Biblical Greek and his website billmounce.com has resources, but it is geared towards those who have purchased his textbook.

Another free online textbook to check out is Biblical Greek: Beginning the Adventure by Nathan E. Purtzer.
