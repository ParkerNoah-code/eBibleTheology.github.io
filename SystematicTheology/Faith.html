Faith is a Gift

A. Faith as a Result of God's Grace

Ephesians 2:8-10

"For by grace you have been saved through faith, and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God, not of works, lest anyone should boast. For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand that we should walk in them."

This passage explicitly states that faith is a gift from God. Salvation does not come from human effort or merit but is entirely the result of God’s grace. Since faith itself is included in this divine gift, it is not something that originates from human willpower or decision but from God’s sovereign bestowal.

Titus 3:5

"Not by works of righteousness which we have done, but according to His mercy He saved us, through the washing of regeneration and renewing of the Holy Spirit."

Here, salvation is attributed solely to God's mercy rather than human righteousness. This underscores that faith, being part of salvation, is not achieved by personal effort but is granted by God through the regenerating work of the Holy Spirit.

Romans 9:16

"So then it is not of him who wills, nor of him who runs, but of God who shows mercy."

This verse reinforces the teaching that salvation—and consequently faith—is not based on human decision or exertion but is entirely dependent on God’s mercy. Faith is not something one musters up but is sovereignly granted by God.

B. Faith as God’s Sovereign Work in Believers

Philippians 1:6

"Being confident of this very thing, that He who has begun a good work in you will complete it until the day of Jesus Christ."

Paul assures believers that the faith initiated in them is a work of God, which He will bring to completion. This confirms that faith is not self-generated but is divinely originated and sustained.

Philippians 1:29

"For to you it has been granted on behalf of Christ, not only to believe in Him, but also to suffer for His sake."

This verse explicitly states that belief in Christ is something that is "granted" to believers. This indicates that faith is a gift given by God rather than something human beings produce on their own.

Philippians 2:12-13

"Work out your own salvation with fear and trembling; for it is God who works in you both to will and to do for His good pleasure."

While believers are called to actively live out their salvation, this passage clarifies that it is ultimately God who is at work within them, both in their will and in their actions. This divine empowerment includes the faith necessary to believe and obey.

2 Peter 1:1, 3

"To those who have obtained like precious faith with us by the righteousness of our God and Savior Jesus Christ… as His divine power has given to us all things that pertain to life and godliness, through the knowledge of Him who called us by glory and virtue."

Faith is described as something "obtained" rather than self-generated. It is received through the righteousness of Christ and granted by His divine power, emphasizing that faith is given, not earned.

C. Faith as a Work of the Holy Spirit

John 6:29

"Jesus answered and said to them, ‘This is the work of God, that you believe in Him whom He sent.’"

Jesus makes it clear that belief in Him is the work of God, not merely a human choice. Faith is therefore not something that originates from human initiative but is a divine work.

John 6:44-45

"No one can come to Me unless the Father who sent Me draws him; and I will raise him up at the last day. It is written in the prophets, ‘And they shall all be taught by God.’ Therefore everyone who has heard and learned from the Father comes to Me."

Jesus explicitly states that no one can come to Him unless drawn by the Father. This indicates that faith is the result of God's initiative, not human decision. The drawing of the Father is essential for belief.

John 6:51, 63

"I am the living bread which came down from heaven. If anyone eats of this bread, he will live forever; and the bread that I shall give is My flesh, which I shall give for the life of the world… It is the Spirit who gives life; the flesh profits nothing. The words that I speak to you are spirit, and they are life."

Christ affirms that the Spirit gives life, meaning that faith and spiritual understanding come from the Spirit, not human ability. The flesh alone cannot produce saving faith.

1 Corinthians 2:4, 14

"And my speech and my preaching were not with persuasive words of human wisdom, but in demonstration of the Spirit and of power… But the natural man does not receive the things of the Spirit of God, for they are foolishness to him; nor can he know them, because they are spiritually discerned."

Paul explains that faith does not come through human reasoning or persuasion but through the Spirit’s power. Without the Spirit, a person cannot understand or accept spiritual truths, showing that faith is a divine work.

D. Faith as the Result of God's Call and Election

Acts 13:48

"Now when the Gentiles heard this, they were glad and glorified the word of the Lord. And as many as had been appointed to eternal life believed."

This passage clearly states that those who believed had been "appointed to eternal life." Faith follows God’s appointment, showing that belief is a result of His sovereign choice.

Romans 8:27-30

"Now He who searches the hearts knows what the mind of the Spirit is, because He makes intercession for the saints according to the will of God… whom He foreknew, He also predestined… called… justified… glorified."

Faith is part of God’s predetermined plan, beginning with His foreknowledge and culminating in justification and glorification. This shows that faith is not random or self-generated but is part of God's sovereign work.

2 Thessalonians 2:13-14

"But we are bound to give thanks to God always for you, brethren beloved by the Lord, because God from the beginning chose you for salvation through sanctification by the Spirit and belief in the truth, to which He called you by our gospel, for the obtaining of the glory of our Lord Jesus Christ."

Faith is connected to God’s calling and election. Believers come to faith because they were chosen by God for salvation, reinforcing that faith is a divine gift.

E. Faith as New Birth Through God's Initiative

John 1:12-13

"But as many as received Him, to them He gave the right to become children of God, to those who believe in His name: who were born, not of blood, nor of the will of the flesh, nor of the will of man, but of God."

Faith and new birth are attributed to God’s will, not human effort. This confirms that faith originates from God's initiative.

John 3:6-8

"That which is born of the flesh is flesh, and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit… The wind blows where it wishes, and you hear the sound of it, but cannot tell where it comes from and where it goes. So is everyone who is born of the Spirit."

Jesus emphasizes that spiritual birth, which includes faith, is a work of the Holy Spirit, not human will.

1 John 5:1

"Whoever believes that Jesus is the Christ is born of God, and everyone who loves Him who begot also loves him who is begotten of Him."

Faith is a sign of being born of God, meaning that it follows regeneration, rather than preceding it.

James 1:18

"Of His own will He brought us forth by the word of truth, that we might be a kind of firstfruits of His creatures."

Faith is the result of God’s will in bringing about spiritual birth through His word.
